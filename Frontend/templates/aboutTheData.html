Why This Data?

  • We have a mutual curiosity in renewable energy

  • This data is comprehenisve as it records each States' monthly/quarterly energy generation from 2001 through 2021, and their specific categories of production

  • With the decrease in the costs of renewable energy and energy storage, the combination of renewable energy generation and storage to replace fossil fuels is more economically feasible. Therefore, it can be predicted that the database we have chosen will be required by various professions.

Our Process

  • We began with a CSV containing the monthly energy production of each state in 2021 before converting itinto SQL tables

  • This data required cleaning and organizing to suit our project's needs

  • We swapped the column labels from month, each state with its category of production to location, category of production, and months from January to December

  • We found this format was more readable than its original format

A Note on Total Annual Output and Total Annual Renewable Output

  • There are some states for which the Total Annual Renewable output is greater than the total annual output

  • We are not completly sure why this is, but we know it has something to do with the original data

  • We calculate total annual ouput from the original variable "All fuels" in the original data set

  • We calculate total annual renewable output from the sums of all renewable categories in the data set

  • We theorize that "All fuels" may just contain the total energy produced in a state that stays in a state and that all the other renewable categories (from which we calculate Total Annual Renewable Output) may also include energy produced in that state that is exported to other states
